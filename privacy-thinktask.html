Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 September 2025

ThinkTask (“we”, “us”, “the App”) turns speech into an actionable checklist. This policy explains what data the App handles, how it is used, and your choices. It reflects the current implementation using Apple Speech Recognition and Google’s Generative Language (Gemini) API.

No accounts Local storage No ads / analytics

Summary

  • We don’t run servers for user content. Your transcripts and plans are stored on your device only.
  • Audio stays on device for transcription. Apple’s speech engine may process on-device or via Apple’s servers depending on your locale and settings.
  • When you tap Go!, the text transcript (not audio) is sent directly from your device to Google’s Gemini API to generate a plan.
  • No ads, no third-party analytics, and no data sales.

Data we store on your device

The App saves content inside its iOS/iPadOS sandbox:

  • Offline queue entries you save manually.
  • Game plans (title, summary, checklist items, notes, original transcript text).

For durability the app writes to Documents and mirrors to Application Support with a shadow copy in UserDefaults (e.g. plans.v1.json, savedQueue.v1.json). Delete items in-app or uninstall to erase the container.

Audio & speech recognition

  • Microphone access is used to capture your voice.
  • Transcription uses Apple’s SFSpeechRecognizer. Depending on device/language, iOS may process on-device or via Apple services governed by Apple’s privacy policies and your settings (e.g., “Enable Dictation”).
  • You can import .m4a files from the Files app for transcription. The App does not upload these files.
  • The App does not permanently store audio; it’s handled transiently for transcription.

AI plan generation (Gemini)

  • Only the transcript text you choose to send is transmitted to Google’s Generative Language API to produce a structured plan.
  • Requests go directly from your device to Google (no intermediary developer server in this version).
  • Processing may occur on servers outside your country. Please review Google’s applicable privacy terms for the Generative Language API.

What we do not collect

  • No account details (name, email, phone).
  • No precise location, contacts, photos, or calendars.
  • No advertising identifiers; no third-party analytics SDKs.

Permissions

  • Microphone — capture audio for transcription.
  • Speech Recognition — convert speech to text via Apple’s engine.
  • Files & Media (optional) — import voice notes you choose from the Files app.

Your choices & controls

  • Delete items via in-app actions (swipe to delete, Clear All in Saved, etc.).
  • Revoke permissions anytime in iOS Settings.
  • Uninstall the App to erase all local data.
  • If you shared content to other apps via the iOS Share Sheet, manage or delete it within those apps.

Legal bases (GDPR/UK GDPR)

  • Consent — for microphone and speech recognition permissions.
  • Performance of a contract — to provide core features you request (transcription, plan generation, saving items).

California (CCPA/CPRA)

We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. You can request access or deletion of any developer-held information by emailing support@thinktask.com. (For Apple-processed speech data or Google AI processing, refer to those providers’ policies.)

Children’s privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided data, contact us at support@thinktask.com.

Security

Your data lives inside the iOS/iPadOS sandbox. Devices with a passcode benefit from hardware-backed encryption. Network requests to Google use TLS; however, no internet service is 100% secure.

International transfers

Google may process the text you submit to its AI service on servers located worldwide. By using the AI feature you consent to this transfer.

Retention

Content remains on your device until you delete it or remove the App. We do not keep a server copy.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the App evolves (e.g., if cloud sync, accounts, or analytics are added). We’ll post updates here and adjust the “Last updated” date.
